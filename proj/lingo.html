Getting to Know The "Lingo"

Crochet patterns use abbreviations and symbols to tell you what steps to take. It might seem overwhelming at first, but don't worry! We've got you covered.

Here's a list of some of the most common crochet terms and what they mean:
shorthand name what it does
CH Chain Stitch The foundation of most crochet projects
MR Magic Circle or Magic Ring The foundation for starting a crochet project in a circle or “in the round”
SC Single Crochet A simple stitch that’s used in most projects
HDC Half Double Crochet A versatile stitch that’s great for creating soft fabrics
DC Double Crochet A taller stitch that adds texture and drape
SL ST Slip Stitch A stitch used to join rounds or move across stitches
INC Increase This refers to inserting multiple stitches into the same pre-existing stitch
DEC Decrease This refers to joining two stitches together in order to decrease the number of stitches
BLO Back Loop Only Inserting stitches into the back loop, used either to create a base for a project or a line
TOG Together Used following a term and a number- Refers to performing a stitch over a set number of preexisting stitches
Yarn Over Looping the yarn over your hook before going into a stitch
